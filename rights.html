Know Your Rights

You have the right to dignified, unbiased, and equitable care.

As a Black woman navigating the healthcare system, knowing your legal rights is one of the most powerful tools you have. You are protected by federal law — and when those protections are violated, you have the right to speak up and demand accountability.

Your Legal Protections

Title VI of the Civil Rights Act (1964)

Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, or national origin in any healthcare program or activity that receives federal funding. This applies to virtually every hospital, clinic, and healthcare provider in the United States.

Medicaid Patient Rights

If you are on Medicaid you cannot be denied care or treated differently because of your race. You are entitled to the same quality of care as any other patient. Your provider must offer free language assistance if English is not your first language.

Right to Pain Management

You have the right to have your pain taken seriously and treated appropriately. Racialized myths about pain tolerance have no place in medical care. If your pain is dismissed or minimized you have the right to seek a second opinion and file a complaint.

If You Are Being Ignored in an Emergency

If you are in a medical emergency and feel your concerns are being ignored:

  • Ask to speak to the charge nurse or attending physician immediately
  • Say clearly: "I am not being heard and I need immediate attention"
  • Request that your concerns be documented in your medical record
  • If discharged and still in pain — return or go to another facility
